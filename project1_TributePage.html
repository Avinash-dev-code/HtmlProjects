Dr.Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr.Norman Borlaug,third from the left train biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields-part of this life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr.Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco,Jowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University Of Minnesca,thanks to Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Finish university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service/li>
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson.Gets laid off due to budget cuts.Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman ,her returns to school study under Stakman who teaches him about breeding pre-resistent of plants
  • 1944 - Reject 100% salary increase from Dupoint,leaves behind his pregnant wife.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season doubling wheat yields
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indian subcontinent in time to help mass stravation due to a rapidly expanding popuation
  • 1970 - recevies the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - helps seven African countries dramatically increase thier maize and sorghum yeilds
  • 2005 - states"we will have to double the world food supply by 2050."Argues that geneically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand,as we run out arable land.
  • 2009 - dies at the age of 95
